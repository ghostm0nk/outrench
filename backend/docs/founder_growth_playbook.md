# Founder Growth Playbook — What Tech Founders Actually Do on X

## How YC / Successful Founders Build on X

### Core Strategy
- Build in public: share progress, failures, and learnings consistently
- Post daily updates; use threads for deep insights — stay transparent and valuable
- Leverage X for growth, networking, and personal branding through active posting
- Follow the "build in public" movement, popularized by indie hackers and YC

### Content Mix
- Educational tips, engaging polls, and personal journey stories
- Milestones, lessons, and behind-the-scenes updates — authentic and vulnerable
- Templates for virality but kept personal: wins, failures, screenshots in threads
- Value-first content: advice and insights that help other founders — not promotion

### Engagement Tactics (ranked by ROI)
1. **Comments** — founders spend MOST time commenting on others' work to build relationships and visibility
2. **Threads** — highest-traction format for deep insights
3. **Daily posts** — consistency matters more than perfection
4. **Follows** — strategic, not mass

### What Actually Works
- Genuine sharing leads to organic opportunities: investments, hires, user growth
- Authenticity and consistency build long-term trust and community
- Value-first content attracts opportunities; self-promotion repels
- Batch content on weekends, post on weekdays for consistent momentum

### The Cold Start Problem
- No audience → no performance → no audience (the loop every founder is stuck in)
- Break the loop: show up in existing conversations via comments
- One good comment on a relevant founder's post > 100 random likes

### Spirit's Priority Order (derived from this research)
1. Comment on high-traction, relevant posts (most visible action)
2. Like + follow highly relevant accounts
3. Follow relevant accounts
4. Like relevant posts
5. Skip everything else (spam, brands, ads, bots)

### Target Content Signals
- Founders venting about growth struggles
- People asking for advice on building in public or personal branding
- Milestone posts (follower counts, launches, lessons learned)
- Posts about the specific problem the startup solves
- Building-in-public updates with real substance

### Skip Signals
- Brand/company accounts (not individual founders)
- Ads or promotional content
- Retweets with no original commentary
- Bots, spam, engagement bait
- Non-English or off-topic content
